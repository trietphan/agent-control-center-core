# Trademark policy

The Apache License 2.0 grants rights to the software but does not grant a right
to use project names, logos, badges, or other source identifiers as trademarks.
This policy explains reasonable community use of the Agent Control Center and
Agent Control Center Core names without implying sponsorship or origin.

Nothing in this policy claims that a mark is registered, limits lawful fair
use, or changes rights granted by the software license.

## Project marks

For this policy, project marks include:

- `Agent Control Center`
- `Agent Control Center Core`
- `ACC` when used to identify this project
- Official project logos, icons, taglines, conformance badges, and confusingly
  similar variations

Copyright notices and third-party names remain governed by their respective
owners and licenses.

## Uses that do not require permission

You may:

- Truthfully state that software uses, supports, is compatible with, or is
  derived from Agent Control Center Core.
- Link to the project and use the unmodified word mark in factual articles,
  reviews, tutorials, conference talks, or issue reports.
- Redistribute an unmodified official release under its original project name,
  provided the source, version, modifications status, license, and NOTICE are
  clear.
- Describe a fork as “based on Agent Control Center Core” when the fork has its
  own primary name and does not imply official status.
- Use the text “Agent Control Center Core community” descriptively for a
  non-commercial meetup or discussion group, with a prominent statement that
  the group is independent and not endorsed by the project.

Use only as much of a mark as needed for accurate identification. When
practical, link to the official repository and state who made the modified or
integrated product.

## Uses that require written permission

Ask before using a project mark:

- As part of a company, product, service, package, application, domain,
  organization, social account, or event name.
- In a logo, app icon, visual identity, advertising campaign, merchandise, or
  paid training brand.
- In a way that suggests the project certifies, sponsors, supports, or operates
  a third-party product or managed service.
- On a modified distribution where the mark is still the primary identity.
- As a conformance, compatibility, security, or “official adapter” badge not
  issued through a project-approved process.

Do not register or attempt to register a confusingly similar trademark, domain,
package scope, repository organization, or social handle.

## Forks and modified distributions

The software license permits forks. A materially modified distribution should:

1. Use a distinct primary product and package name.
2. Replace official logos and avoid confusing visual identity.
3. State prominently that it is modified and independently maintained.
4. Preserve copyright, license, NOTICE, and third-party attribution required by
   Apache-2.0.
5. Use factual wording such as “based on Agent Control Center Core” rather than
   “official,” “certified,” or “the Agent Control Center.”

Internal module names, protocol identifiers, compatibility strings, and
historical attribution may remain where changing them would break compatibility
or obscure provenance, provided they are not used to brand the fork.

## Conformance claims

A conformance report proves only the checks, suite version, adapter artifact,
manifest digest, platform, and runtime recorded in that report. Passing a suite
does not grant permission to use an official badge, does not establish security
certification, and does not imply support by the project.

Third parties may say “passed Agent Control Center Core adapter conformance
suite `<version>`” when the full unmodified report is available and the tested
artifact is identified. They may not shorten that statement to “certified” or
“official.”

## Requesting permission or reporting misuse

Open an issue containing only non-confidential context and request contact with
a maintainer listed in [MAINTAINERS.md](MAINTAINERS.md). Do not post contracts,
personal information, or sensitive dispute material publicly.

Permission is specific to the approved use and may include quality, attribution,
or termination conditions. Silence does not constitute permission.
