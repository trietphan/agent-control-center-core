# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in Agent Control Center Core a safe,
respectful, and productive experience for everyone, regardless of background,
identity, experience level, location, or role.

Technical disagreement is expected. Harassment, intimidation, and personal
attacks are not.

## Expected behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Discuss ideas, evidence, and tradeoffs without attacking people.
- Assume good intent while remaining precise about safety and correctness.
- Give actionable feedback and accept correction without retaliation.
- Respect privacy, credentials, customer data, and responsible-disclosure
  boundaries.
- Credit others' work and disclose relevant conflicts of interest.
- Help make issues, reviews, meetings, and community spaces accessible to
  newcomers.
- Stop behavior that another participant reasonably identifies as unwelcome.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behavior is unacceptable in project spaces:

- Harassment, threats, stalking, discriminatory language, or sexualized
  attention.
- Trolling, sustained disruption, deliberate humiliation, or repeated personal
  attacks.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Pressuring someone to reveal credentials, employer information, customer
  data, security findings, or private communications.
- Knowingly submitting malicious code, hidden telemetry, credential collection,
  deceptive fixtures, or fabricated verification evidence.
- Retaliating against a person who raises a good-faith concern.
- Other conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a
  professional open-source community.

## Scope

This policy applies in repositories, issues, pull requests, code review,
project meetings, chat spaces, release activity, and public events where a
participant is representing the project. It also applies to private
communications when they directly affect a person's ability to participate in
the project safely.

This policy governs the open-source project community. Separately operated
services may publish additional customer and workplace policies.

## Reporting

Do not post sensitive conduct reports in a public issue.

Use the repository's private **Report a vulnerability** channel when available
and prefix the report title with `CODE OF CONDUCT`. This use of a private channel
does not characterize the report as a software vulnerability. If that channel
is unavailable, contact a non-involved maintainer through the public contact
method on their GitHub profile and ask for a private reporting channel without
including incident details publicly. Current maintainers are listed in
[MAINTAINERS.md](MAINTAINERS.md).

Include, when safe:

- What happened and where.
- Relevant dates, links, screenshots, or witnesses.
- Whether the behavior is continuing or creates an immediate safety concern.
- Any conflict that should cause a maintainer to recuse.

For software vulnerabilities, follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) instead.

## Enforcement process

A non-conflicted maintainer will acknowledge a report within five business days
when a working private contact channel exists. Maintainers will limit report
details to people needed to investigate, give the reported person a fair chance
to respond when safe, document the decision, and protect reporters from
retaliation.

Proportionate actions may include:

1. A private clarification or warning.
2. Required correction, apology, or temporary communication limits.
3. Removal of content or temporary suspension from project spaces.
4. Permanent removal from project spaces or maintainer responsibilities.
5. Escalation to the relevant hosting provider or authorities when required for
   safety or law.

The project may act immediately to contain credible threats, exposed private
information, or active abuse. A person affected by an enforcement decision may
request reconsideration by a non-conflicted maintainer within 30 days. The
review outcome is final for project spaces.

Maintainers who violate this policy are subject to the same standards and must
recuse from handling reports in which they are involved.
